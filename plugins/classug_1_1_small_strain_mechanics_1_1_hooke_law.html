Detailed Description

template<typename TDomain>
class ug::SmallStrainMechanics::HookeLaw< TDomain >

The classical material law to describe linear elastic behavior for isotropic, homogeneous materials. This law is based on the assumption of small strains, since only the linearized strain tensor (commonly denoted with ) is used. With $u$ being the displacement vector, the strain tensor is defined by

= {1}{2}( u + ( u)^T),

i.e. higher orders of the gradient of u are neglected here (-> 'linearized strain tensor').

Additionally Hooke`s law assumes a linear connection of stresses and strains. Therefore, the stresses are described by a law of the form,

= C :

with C being a constant tensor of rank 4, called 'ElasticityTensor'. denotes the cauchy-stress tensor, which is, just like the strain tensor , a symmetric tensor of rank 2. ':' denotes the tensor contraction, in components,

{ij} = C_{ijkl} {kl}.
